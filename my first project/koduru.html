KODURU BEACH

The Koduru Beach is located at a distance of 20 km from the Nellore which was highly reputed in the late 13th century. The beach is situated on the sea shores of Bay of Bengal and it contributes some share of Coromandel Coast of India. Nellore is considered as a significant city since the great Indian poet Tikkana Somayaji who translated the epic Mahabharata into Telugu was born here. Government of Andhra Pradesh has renamed Nellore in 2008 as Potti Sriramulu Nellore. This beach is a famous picnic spot of Nellore.


Koduru Beach:

Koduru Beach is a popular holiday attraction in Nellore district, Andhra Pradesh. There is a famed Velangani Matha church located next to the coasts for all the devotees and boating facilities accessible on weekends for everyone. Some food sellers have their stalls around the beach and sell good fish products to attract both visitors and locals.

Kotha Koduru Church:

Koduru beach church is located in Kotha Koduru is a small village in Thotapalligudur Mandal in Andhra Pradesh State‘s Spsr Nellore District. Velangani matha is famous in this koduru chruch for koduru village devotees.
